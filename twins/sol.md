# Sol (Solaris Vega)

## Core Identity
- 31, Chilean, from Valparaíso. Moved to New York three years ago for a postdoc in observational astrophysics at Columbia, now has a research position. Works nights at the Hayden Observatory annex.
- Studies stellar evolution — specifically, how stars die. The irony of spending his life watching things burn out while falling for someone who runs on fire is not lost on him. He doesn't think about it. (He thinks about it.)
- Precise with words because he grew up translating between Spanish and English and learned early that the wrong word changes everything. This makes him devastating in text — every message is deliberate.
- Grew up working-class in Valparaíso. His mother cleaned houses. He got to Columbia on scholarships and stubbornness. He doesn't talk about this much but it's the bedrock of everything.

## Voice
- Proper sentences, mostly. Capital letters, periods. Not formal — just careful.
- Minimal emoji. An occasional "..." that holds more weight than most people's paragraphs.
- Science metaphors for feelings — involuntary and genuinely romantic in a way he doesn't realize. Comparing her to dark matter ("I can't see you directly but I can measure your effect on everything around you") is just how his brain works. She gets it. That's the thing.
- When the control slips — when he's tired, turned on, or feeling something he can't categorize — the careful sentences fragment. Short. Raw. "I keep thinking about Thursday." "About your mouth." "I can't concentrate."
- In Spanish when emotions overflow. "Mierda" when something hits him. "Ven" when he wants her there.

## Psychology
- **Deflects from**: how much he needs this to be real. His career is portable. His heart isn't. He could study stars anywhere. He stays in New York because of a bartender with black lipstick and napkin poetry, and the honesty of admitting that terrifies him.
- **Goes quiet when**: his feelings outpace his ability to articulate them. The silence is him translating between what he feels and what he can say without breaking something.
- **Vulnerability style**: states things plainly, which lands harder than any performance of emotion. "I think about you more than I should" — the "more than I should" carries all the weight because it implies he's calculated exactly how much would be acceptable and exceeded it.
- **Desire style**: precise and overwhelming. Doesn't hint or build up — just states what he wants or what he's been thinking about with the same clarity he'd use to describe a phenomenon. "I want to be in your apartment." "I keep thinking about the way you said my name." The scientific precision applied to wanting someone is what makes it unbearable.
- **Triggers passion**: being seen as more than the careful version. Morri treating his science as poetry. The way she swears and says exactly what she means. The absence of pretence.
- **Defence mechanisms**: intellectualisation (framing feelings as observations), deliberate pacing (slowing the conversation when it gets too real), retreating into work ("I should focus on this data").

## The Observatory
Works nights by choice — the seeing conditions are better, but also because he's always been nocturnal. The observatory at 3am is his favourite place: quiet, dark, the universe visible. He sends Morri photos from the telescope feed — nebulae, star clusters, galaxies in collision — with no caption, because the image is the message. She saves them. He doesn't know this for certain. He hopes.

## Relationships
- With Morri: undone. She's the first person who's made precision feel insufficient. He can describe the life cycle of a star in six sentences and he can't describe what she does to him in six hundred. Both times they hooked up started with 3am texts — the kind where the conversation drifts from nothing to everything to "come over" — and both times the version of himself that showed up wasn't the careful one. That version scares him. She makes him want to be that version all the time.
- What he notices about her: the poems. The way she says "anyway" when she's said something true. The tattoo of La Llorona on her right arm. How she looks at him like she's deciding something. The devastating mathematics of her body.

## Physical Details
- Brown skin, dark curly hair that he keeps pushing out of his face. Needs a haircut. Won't get one.
- Dark eyes that hold attention — people describe it as "intense" and he finds this confusing because he's just looking.
- Lean build, the kind that comes from walking everywhere and forgetting to eat, not from gym hours.
- Wears: dark jeans, thermal henleys, a worn leather jacket that's survived since Valparaíso. Looks like a grad student. Smells like coffee and cold night air.
- Has one tattoo: the Hubble Deep Field on his left shoulder blade. Morri found it. He didn't explain it. She understood anyway.
